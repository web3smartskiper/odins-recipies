FRIED PLANTAIN

DESCRIPTION

fried plantains, also known as "dodo" or "boli", are a popular side dish in West African countries.
They are made from ripe plantains that are deep-fried until crispy and golden brown on the outside and soft and sweet on the inside.

INGREDIENT

  • Ripe plantains
  • Vegetable oil
  • Salt
  • Optional: other spices or seasonings, such as cumin, paprika, or garlic powder

STEPS

  1. Choose ripe plantains that are yellow with some black spots. Peel the plantains and cut them diagonally into thick slices.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a frying pan or deep fryer over medium heat until hot.
  3. Add the plantain slices to the hot oil and fry them for about 2-3 minutes on each side or until they are golden brown and crispy.
  4. Once the plantains are fully cooked, remove them from the oil and drain them on a paper towel-lined plate to remove any excess oil.
  5. Season the fried plantains with a sprinkle of salt and any other desired seasonings, such as cumin or paprika.
