FRIED RICE

DESCRIPTION

Fried Rice is a rice dish that is popular in Nigeria and is typically made with long-grain
rice, mixed vegetables, and a variety of meats such as chicken, beef, or shrimp.
It is seasoned with a variety of traditional Nigerian spices and seasonings, such as curry
powder,thyme, garlic, ginger, and salt, giving it a unique flavor profile. It is a delicious
and satisfying meal that can be enjoyed on its own or as a side dish with other Nigerian dishes.

INGREDIENT

  • Long-grain rice
  • Mixed vegetables such as carrots, green beans, and peas
  • Meat such as chicken, beef, or shrimp (optional)
  • Vegetable oil or butter for saut√©ing
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Curry powder
  • Thyme
  • Salt and pepper
  • Soy sauce (optional)
  • Chicken or beef broth (optional)

STEPS

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes. Drain the water and set aside.
  2. Chop the vegetables and meat into small pieces and set aside.
  3. Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat and add the vegetable oil or butter.
  4. Add the chopped onions, garlic, and ginger and stir-fry for about 1-2 minutes or until fragrant.
  5. Add the chopped meat (if using) and stir-fry until browned and cooked through.
  6. Add the chopped vegetables and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes or until they are slightly softened but still crisp.
  7. Add the rice to the skillet or wok and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes or until the rice is heated through.
  8. Add the curry powder, thyme, salt, and pepper, and stir to combine.
  9. If desired, add soy sauce or broth to the skillet or wok and stir-fry for another minute or two until the rice is evenly coated with the sauce. Serve hot and enjoy!
